# NNNN: Short, decision-stated title

State the decision in the title itself — "Use Gemini File Search for RAG," not "RAG approach."

## Context

What situation forced this decision? What were the constraints (cost, timeline, team size, existing dependencies)? A reader with none of the background you had should understand why a decision was even needed.

## Decision

What did we actually decide, stated plainly. If there were alternatives seriously considered, name them and say why they lost — that's often the most useful part of an ADR, since it stops the same alternative from being re-litigated later without new information.

## Consequences

What does this make easier? What does it make harder or foreclose? What follow-up work or risk did we knowingly accept? Be honest about downsides — an ADR that only lists benefits isn't trusted the next time it's read.
